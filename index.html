Why Mangroves Matter

Coastal Protection

Mangroves act as natural barriers, protecting coastlines from storms, tsunamis, and erosion while reducing flood risks for millions of people.

  • • Reduce wave energy by up to 70%
  • • Prevent coastal erosion naturally
  • • Buffer against storm surges

Carbon Storage

These ecosystems store 3-5 times more carbon than tropical rainforests, making them crucial allies in fighting climate change.

  • • Store up to 10x more carbon per hectare
  • • Sequester carbon for centuries
  • • Help mitigate climate change

Marine Nurseries

Over 80% of global fish species depend on mangrove ecosystems for breeding, feeding, and shelter during critical life stages.

  • • Support 80% of global fisheries
  • • Provide nursery habitats
  • • Maintain biodiversity
